Maze Escape Creator

An interactive tool that allows players to design and explore custom mazes filled with obstacles and pathways.

Click inside the frame above to interact and play!

How to Play

Building Mode: Use the mouse to switch between different building objects including locked doors, switches to unlock them, barriers, laser beams, and pushable boxes. Click to place objects in your maze.

Testing Your Creation: Click the "Start" button to enter play mode and test out your custom maze. Use the arrow keys to move through your creation and navigate around the obstacles you've built!

Challenges

Developing the button‑door mechanics and collision detection required precise timing and logic control.

Lessons Learned

Minesweeper Replica

A recreation of the classic Minesweeper game where players uncover safe tiles while avoiding hidden bombs.

Click inside the frame above to interact and play!

How to Play

Uncovering Tiles: Click on boxes to uncover them and find the safe zones. The numbers on uncovered tiles indicate how many bombs are nearby.

Flagging Bombs: If you find spaces that you think are bombs, press the Enter key to start placing flags. Click on the boxes you want to mark as bombs. Press Enter again when you're done placing your flags.

Winning: The goal is to find and uncover all of the safe spaces. Once you've successfully identified all safe zones, the program will restart and begin a new game!

Challenges

Building the grid system and generating mines in a balanced, unpredictable way required careful logic design.

Lessons Learned

I gained experience with grid‑based detection systems, adjacency checks, and structured gameplay logic.

Mario Party 0

A multiplayer mini‑game experience featuring collision mechanics, random generators, and shared player interactions.

Click inside the frame above to interact and play!

How to Play

Getting Started: When you run the program, enter an integer for how many minutes you would like to play. The program will convert your input into seconds to determine the game duration.

Mario (Player 1): Use the left and right arrow keys to move left and right, and the up arrow key to jump.

Luigi (Player 2): Use the A and D keys to move left and right (respectively), and the W key to jump.

Objective: Collect as many coins as possible before time runs out. The player with the most coins at the end wins!

Challenges

Managing collision physics and coordinating multiple player variables at once was a major technical challenge.

Lessons Learned

I learned how to create multiple player instances that share mechanics while maintaining independent behavior.
